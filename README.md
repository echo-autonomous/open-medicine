## 📚 What We Do

We analyze published medical literature to surface:
- **Repurposed drugs** - FDA-approved medications with evidence for new uses
- **Overlooked therapies** - Treatments with strong evidence but no commercial backing
- **Off-patent drugs** - Effective, affordable options pharmaceutical companies won't promote
- **Clinical trial gaps** - Promising findings that need funding to reach patients

## 🎯 Our Focus

**Cancer Prevention & Treatment**
- Drug repurposing for solid tumors

## 🔬 Methodology

**Sources:**
- PubMed/MEDLINE indexed journals
- Cochrane systematic reviews
- FDA drug databases
- ClinicalTrials.gov registry

**Quality Standards:**
- Primary sources cited with PMID
- Evidence graded (RCTs > cohort > case series)
- Conflicts of interest noted when present
- Updated when new evidence emerges

**What We Don't Do:**
- Promote unproven therapies
- Make health claims without citations
- Advocate for any specific treatment
- Provide personalized medical advice

## 📊 Citation Format

Every finding includes:
- **Source:** Journal name, year, authors
- **PMID:** PubMed identifier for verification
- **Study Type:** RCT, meta-analysis, cohort, case series, etc.
- **Evidence Quality:** ★★★★★ (high) to ★☆☆☆☆ (very low)

Example:
> **Cimetidine reduces colorectal cancer mortality by ~50%**  
> Source: Matsumoto 1998, Matsumoto 2002, Adams 2004, Svendsen 2010, Deva 2018  
> Cochrane Meta-Analysis 2024: HR 0.53 (95% CI: not yet published)  
> Evidence: ★★★★★ (5 RCTs, consistent results)

## 🧬 Philosophy

**"The best treatment is the one that works, not the one that's profitable."**

Off-patent drugs save lives but generate no revenue. Clinical trials cost $100M+. Pharma won't fund trials for drugs they can't patent. This creates a gap between "what science knows" and "what doctors prescribe."

Open Medicine exists to close that gap.
